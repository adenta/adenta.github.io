Indy 500 Dashboard

May 29, 2016

What is this?

My family likes to have a contest to see who can get the most predictions right for the Indy 500. I wrote a quick dashboard, and we were able to see in real time who was ‘winning’ based on our picks. This page used to point at the live Indy 500 endpoint, but it is currently switched to a dummy server I set up on Heroku to serve some test data, that we can use next year if we want to modify this. I did not do so well, The guy I picked to win was out at lap 64.

How does scoring work?

For the 2016 Indy 500, you picked three racers. You received six points if you guessed who came in first, three points if you guessed who came in second place or better, and you received one point if you guessed who came in third place or better. This system worked, but is not what the dashboard currently reflects.

New scoring system

I was playing around with alternative scoring systems, and arrived on something I liked better. In the alternative system seen below, each participant picks who they thought would land in the top three. For each racer a participant chose, we take the total number of racers, and subtract the placing the racer achieved. This gives us a metric with a minimum value of six and a maximum of three times the number of racers. This shows how good each participant is at guessing who will do well in the race.
